Research

Adaptive Mesh Refinement (AMR) in GRMHD
Developing and implementing novel Adaptive Mesh Refinement (AMR) criteria in the GPU-accelerated astrophysical code, AthenaK. This work targets accretion disk-jet systems and uses a fourth-derivative-based trigger to detect high-frequency oscillations, achieving significant computational speedups while preserving physical accuracy.
Learn more
GRMHD Simulations of Black Hole Accretion Disks
Investigating the physical origin of aperiodic variability in black hole X-ray binaries. This research involves running 2D and 3D GRMHD simulations of accretion disks using the HARMPI code on the Quest cluster and building Python-based analysis pipelines to characterize variability in accretion flows.
Learn more
Protoplanetary Disk Formation and Morphology
Analyzing archival data from the Atacama Large Millimeter/submillimeter Array (ALMA) to investigate the dynamics of collapsing molecular clouds and the formation of protoplanetary disks. This work involved computing disk masses and identifying the morphology of accretion disks.
Learn more
Astrophysical Magnetic Fields in Star-Forming Regions
Reviewing Zeeman diagnostics of astrophysical magnetic fields and examining the role of the Very Long Baseline Array (VLBA) in probing the magnetic fields of star-forming regions like W3(OH).
