About

I am an undergraduate student at DePaul University studying computational astrophysics. My research focuses on understanding high-energy phenomena, particularly compact objects like black holes and their environments, by using and developing large-scale simulations.

I aim to address key challenges in general relativistic magnetohydrodynamics (GRMHD) and numerical relativity by combining physical insight with algorithmic innovation. Through my research experiences at Northwestern University's CIERA and Caltech, I have worked on running GRMHD simulations and advancing the computational frameworks that enable them, such as by developing new Adaptive Mesh Refinement (AMR) techniques.

My goal is to advance our understanding of compact objects by pushing the boundaries of what computational astrophysics can achieve.
